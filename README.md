# U.S-companies-bankruptcy-Analysis

**Project overview and goal.**

This project analyzes financial data from 8,262 American public companies listed on the New York Stock Exchange between 1999 and 2018. The objective is to identify key drivers of bankruptcy and predict whether a company is likely to go bankrupt in the following year

**Business Problem**

Predict which companies are financially distressed and likely to go bankrupt using historical financial data.

**Data Description**

·  Source: Kaggle

·  78,682 records 

·  21 variables

·  Size: 11.25 MB

[Visit Kaggle website](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/utkarshx27/american-companies-bankruptcy-prediction-dataset)

**Key findings**

·  Liquidity is the strongest indicator of bankruptcy risk. 

·  57.27% of distressed companies went bankrupt due primarily to poor liquidity. 

·  9,674 companies were identified in the gray zone and may face bankruptcy within two years. 

·  Bankruptcy rates peaked between 2000 and 2002 before declining.  

**Recommendation**

·  Monitor current ratio and working capital. 

·  Focus on companies with declining liquidity and profitability. 

·  Improve solvency and operational efficiency.

·    Allocate resources to high-risk companies for turn around or wind-up decision

**Limitations of Analysis**

·  No machine learning deployment. 

·  Historical accounting data may lag real-time conditions. 

·  Altman Z-score was applied across industries.
